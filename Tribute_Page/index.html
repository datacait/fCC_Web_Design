Barbara Askins

A visionary inventor

Here are some of the details about Barbara Askins' life and career:

  • Born in Belfast, TN in 1939
  • Returned to college after her children entered school to earn her bachelor's and master's in chemistry
  • Joined NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in 1975
  • Invented the autoradiograph process which produced images with significantly higher contrast and density.
  • The metheod enhanced underexposed emulsions and drastically increased the limits of photographic detection - making visibile that which was formerly invisible
  • Applications for the new method ranged from analysis of images from space to medical technology to photo restoration
  • Awarded the National Inventor of the Year in 1978 by the Association for advancement of Inventions and Innovations.
